Post Sizing

100×100 min. — 125×125 recommended

Outer corner posts (P2, P3, P4) carry the highest lateral loads. Use 125×125 here for significantly better racking resistance at 1400mm height.

Spike Anchor Notes

750 mm drive-in spikes

Suitable in firm, well-compacted ground only. Use concrete footings (750mm deep) if soil is clay or soft.

1
Mark Out & Drive Spikes

String-line all 6 post positions. Drive spikes with a post driver. Check plumb in both axes before each one sets.

2
Set Posts to Height

Insert posts into spikes. Use a laser or water level to mark 1550mm (deck + beam) on every post. Cut to length — recheck level across all posts.

3
Fix Long Rim Beams First

Clamp 2700mm beams to P2→P3 and P1→P6. Level along length. Through-bolt 2× M12 per beam-post joint. Then fit 2300mm beams P4→P3 and P5→P6.

4
Short End Beams & Inner Beams

Fit 600mm beams across P1→P2 and P4→P5. Add the mid-span inner beams to halve joist spans. Secure with joist hangers or connector plates.

5
Install Knee Braces

At P1, P5 and especially P6 fit 45° knee braces (post-to-beam, ~600mm each leg). M10 bolts each end. Critical for racking resistance on the L-shape.

6
Hang Joists

Fix joist hangers to inner rim beam faces at 400mm centres. Drop in 47×100 joists. Check level and flush with beam top.

7
Lay Decking

Fix boards at 90° to joists. 5mm drainage gap using spacers. 2× stainless screws per board per joist. Cut the L inner corner neatly with a jigsaw.

8
Handrail System

Fix 75×75 handrail posts at max 400mm centres on all open edges. Top rail at min. 1100mm above deck. Spindles at max 100mm gaps (children's safety).

9
Finish & Treat

Fit post caps. Apply end-grain preservative to all cut surfaces. Treat all exposed timber with deck oil or stain. Sand all edges for child safety.

10
Inspect & Sign Off

Shake every post, test every rail. Consider a Building Control inspection. Photograph the build. Never allow children on deck before sign-off.
